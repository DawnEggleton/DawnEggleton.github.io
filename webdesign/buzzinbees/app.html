Mobile Application

Our mobile application is intended as a companion application for the Queen Bee neckband device and would be marketed as such. However, it is capable of working on its own. The application itself is a music player with:

  • Basic music player functions - play, pause, skip forward/backward, shuffle, loop, and repeat
  • Song favouriting
  • Custom playlist creation
  • A music visualizer that responds to the bass frequencies of any song that is playing
  • Bluetooth connection to the neckband, when the neckband is present
  • Background functions which send data to the neckband

Below are sections featuring screenshots and development images of our application. Additionally, we have provided details on our application user testing and a video demonstration of our application for your viewing. Lastly, we encourage you to trial our application on your own Android device by downloading our APK.

Application Progression

Home Screen Progression As can be seen below, the home page experienced very few changes from initial conception to final screen. What changes were made were done due to two separate design user tests.


Song List Progression
Similarly to the home page, the song list page experienced very few changes - in fact, even fewer than the home page.


List of Playlists Progression
The list of playlists page experienced more changes than some of the other pages. Namely, the image in advance of the name and the lettered separations were not implemented. The icon seen in the final screenshot is only present for the Favourites playlist, although users can include emojis from their keyboard when titling their playlists.


Add Song to Playlist Progression
The add song to playlist page had the most changes between sketch and wireframe, although the changes between wireframe and final screen were minor.


Visualizer Progression
Note: no exclusive visualizer sketch was done, with the intent of making the visualizer page similar to the 'currently playing' sketch displayed.
